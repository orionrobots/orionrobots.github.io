Good question. Now I will get back to you on the calculations, but the program is an interesting one.

Assuming you are using some kind of Micro Controller, you could use a sensor to detect the position of the lifting arm - either a rotation sensor (if your arm is a jointed kind) or a distance sensor if the arm moves in a linear/parallel way (like a fork lift).

You would then monitor this sensor, and use the feedback from this to control the solenoid assuming you are using solenoids. If the cylinder is double acting - this feedback can be used to manouvre the arm into the right position.

I imagine there are variable pressure valves for fine manouvering, although they may be expensive and difficult to find. However - I have been working on building one of these (at Lego scales - but could be scaled up) with commonly available parts - and will eventually be selling these through ebay and the site.
