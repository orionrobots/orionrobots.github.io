A recently registered user messaged me with a question, which we thought might be better placed on the forums, along with my responses.

Hello
Thanks for accepting my registration.
I enjoyed your site very much as my main interest is in robotics.
I am an electronics technician, and have been teaching myself C++ and Assembly so that I can program my robot with code for various tasks.


Given the small memory footprint, C may be worth learning, but if you are after something that makes more sense to an electronics engineer, you may find the ooPIC worth a look, especially the "virtual circuits" concept it uses. Plus as it comes with objects designed to control servo''s, and on the boards plugs designed to accept them, as well as dealing with PWM, I2C and a myriad of interfacing systems, it may be a tad easier than trying to roll your own all the way.

As I have surplus Z80 CPUs and 2764 EPROMS, I have been trying to design a microcontroller using these components, instead of using dedicated microcontrollers, such as PIC and others.


This shouldnt be a problem, but you will have to consider what you might need to add systems in for IO. You are not going to want to simply wire motors, sensors etc directly into the bus, and probably want some sort of chip just to give you the ports. There are however, chips in the Z80 family which work as single chip microcontrollers? with the digital? and analogue? IO on board. You would also have to program things like PWM control for motors/servos, but you may find that there is library code online to help you there.

I have found it quite a challenge to learn assemly, and although learning the rudimentaries, it is another thing to apply this knowledge to programming an EPROM and writing code for these components.
Do you think I may be better off investing in applying my efforts to using a PIC or other microcontoller?


Okay - I would say to learn what you are comfortable with. Having a surplus of a particular component can be a good reason to put it to use, but what you may find, if the particular die and layout of Z80''s you have do not actually have the IO, that you end up adding in an MCU anyway. Maybe you could take a look here: http://www.z80.info/

I enjoy learning programming, and as an electonics technician, the physical construction will be relatively easier than writing code and programming the 2764 EPROM.
My ultimate goal is to construct an underwater robot for exploration.


Ah - I have had some fun myself doing underwater bots, the linkages can get quite interesting.

Once again, do you think a PIC will be a lot easier for robot control?


I think the ooPIC would be by far the easiest device, but even a PIC will probably be significantly easier to interface with than a basic Z80.

Is using a Z80 and 2764 overkill for robot control?
I''d appreciate your feedback. Thanks
ozwired

Overkill is not really the correct word, its more just design for purpose. Z80 is a more general purpose processor, which has embedded applications but does not AFAIK come with built in''s designed for robotics and similar control engineering projects. However, I think there may be some Z80 based packages which do have those things. Exactly which do you have in your surplus?

Danny
