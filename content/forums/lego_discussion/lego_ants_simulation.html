I have recently been discussing building a colony of RCX or spybotics based ants. I am wandering what ideas people here may have.

What I intend is to use an RCX or Spybotics control for a base station, and have it broadcast a simple repeating message - to say I am here. Then to dot a few of these at some distance from this to represent food. To keep a fairly narrow area for this, I will probably build a structure with this pointing downwards.

The actual ant bots will probably be simple rovers, with a pen at the rear (always down) and a light sensor set up for line following at the front. We need to make sure that the ir transceiver is visible, and incorporate a simple bumper. This is well within the capabilities of a spybot (with their built in sensor) which are much cheaper that the RCX.

The program will use a random number system to pick out a path, and when another line is encountered may choose to follow it. It will keep track of the amount of food (a simple decrementing register) it has, and stop if it has run out. When it has a lot of food - it will seek its base, otherwise it will seek more food. If an ant encounters one without food it can instruct the "dead" bot to follow it back to base and start again - where it will use stored food in the base to revive itself (another counter). Back to base normally will mean to turn roughly 180, and look for the line. If we want to be clever - we could position the pen so it is very close to the center of the turning circle - which would save confusion.

The bumper behaviour could be a simple back off, and turn left or right (randomly determined again).

For the pen ink, we need to try and find some ink that fades away after about 20 minutes, and a good reflective surface.

This experiment would result in interesting behaviour - and would be best filmed in controlled lighting conditions to minimise the IR interference.

If RCX''s are used - a motor could be used to raise the pen when an ant has run out of food. This could be achieved with the Spybot, by having a clutch gear based system opposing a suspension spring. The clutch gear system, when enough forward motion is present, would lower the pen - so the line would be drawn, and when there is not enough forward motion, then the pen would be raised. This could simplify the "dead" behaviour - in that instead of waiting for a mate, it slows down, and "limps" back towards its base - still trying to follow lines. This would mean the errant path is not re-drawn and will fade sooner - optimizing the whole emergent system.

Anyway - its up for discussion - do you have any ideas for this? I would like to keep it all Lego - bar the pen and surface. I am thinking of leaving it a few days and trying about 10-20 ants.

Another thought on this would be to add in "predator" or competing ants. This would complicate the simulation - but would mean having another "nest" for the predators/thieves - who would (using a different kind of line or something) lay their own pheromone trails (the lines) which the ants cant read, read the ants trails, and then go and feed off of ants by going close to them, and using messages to increment their food count while reducing that of their victim.

A victim would again end up limping back, and therefore there would be an emergent tendency (hopefully) in the ants to take a route avoiding the predators. The predators would probably tend to attack both the nest areas, and feeding areas of the ants.
