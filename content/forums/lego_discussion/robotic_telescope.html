I want to build a robotic model telescope like the one used by the National Schools Observatory. I''d like to connect it up to an RCX box for rotation, tilt but not viewing through!

Any ideas? Its for UK National Science Week in March

' date: Thu 24 of Feb., 2005 11:39 GMT - author: orion content: '

After reading a little, it is worth being aware of the fact that the RCX clock has some significant drift, and may require some correction. Sadly - this varies between RCX''s.

You are advised to set the RCX clock, and then check it around 10 hours later to calibrate that drift.

I would also additionally mention that you should probably use worm gears for the turntables to ensure that they dont get easily knocked off course. You could then have a touch sensor along each of the axes triggered by a tooth of some kind so it could position itself in a startup position. This could also be achieved with two rotation sensors - but the touch sensors are much simpler and cheaper.

Anyway - did you actually manage to build one? We would love to see it.

' date: Wed 04 of May, 2005 16:34 BST - author: orion content: '

I would suggest using a large Lego Turntable to mount it on, and building a technic beam frame around the telescope itself (to mount it). For elevation, I would suggest using two large technic gears mounted under the frame, with an axle connecting them to the chassis, and a motor with a fairly large gear reduction for both rotation and elevation.

If you were really sneaky, you could use the RCX''s built in clock to track an object, or light sensors. Let us know how you go, here is a great place to put photos and log your build.
