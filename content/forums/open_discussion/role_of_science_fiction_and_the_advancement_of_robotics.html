Hi there Matt,

A good question, and one I thought about a lot.
I have an old book, one I have kept since I was a kid, it is not in great shape, but it allows me some inspiration. It is named "The Science in Science Fiction" - and it specifically studied what was technobabble and what was real, and which parts of sci fi could become reality any time soon.

The one device that most reminds me of this book is owned by nearly every person in the developed world, and will gain a foothold in the developing world - and that is the mobile phone which outstripped even Star Trek technology of the time this book was printed, by absolutely light years (excuse my pun).

I think you are correct that there is rarely news, but there still is news, and things like ASIMO and QRIO, as well as the Darpa Grand Challenge, the Rep Rap team (who I am yet to write more about) are all things which are early news which possibly predate a much larger shift.

Things seen in sci fi may come to pass, possibly in our lifetimes, but they will take time, and obviously inspiration. There is little better inspiration than sci fi, beyond perhaps studying the natural world and how creatures have evolved to survive in it. Sci fi is one of the driving forces, it is the dreams of many people captured in an easily distributed, entertaining and digestible form, with potential to inspire others as it did the original authors.

I still read Sci fi today written in the 1950''s or even earlier (HG Wells wrote a great deal more than The War Of The Worlds), some which has dated very well and is just as relevant now as it was then.

So in summary, it is very important. Without dreams, we could never reach for the stars. There needs to be a balance between dreamers and doers. Engineers, planners and architects all have there place. If you can not imagine it, if no one can imagine it, it may be all but impossible to achieve something.

Keep on dreaming, and keep on building...

Danny
