' date: Tue 22 of Apr., 2008 20:02 BST - author: Danny Staple content: '

Hi there,
It kind of depends on a few things. You are right basically that motors are more powerful, and that servo''s don''t need a speed controller or H-Bridge. Servo''s simply come with the speed control and bridge included.

Servo''s modified for continuous rotation are used in smaller, lightweight bots, and allow an RC receiver or microcontroller PWM output to directly drive them.

It is worth bearing in mind that while a speed controller adds cost and weight, so do servos, and all told, the cost of two servos vs two motors + a speed controller will not be that far different. The latter is slightly more costly, but will give more performance.

What sort of scale is this robot, and what sort of weight? A rough idea is that the lower end (robots little bigger than RC cars) would lend itself better to the servos, and the higher end to the speed controllers.

One last thing to bear in mind, is that it is possible to salvage DC motors, and build a H-Bridge for the motors yourself. It takes more effort, but you will learn all the more for doing it.

Cheers,
Danny
