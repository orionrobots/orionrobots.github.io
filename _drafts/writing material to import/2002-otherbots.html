Beam Robotics


At the moment Beam robotics have become a little of an obsession, although I am really only in the research stages. but in theory they are robots based on very simple discrete circuits - basic transistors, resistors and caps. The "BICORE" is just a simple Octal Buffer chip - that is a bunch of Not Gates. which essentially act as transistors anyway. If you can understand simple oscillators and biasing transistors for response to LDRs and other sensors you are well on your way to building these. I am currently looking into building a Photovore Hexapod- A Six legged walker which will find light, and use it to charge its batteries. I will keep this site updated with my results and my thoughts.

LapBot


One of the interesting concepts is building a very complicated logic robot - which instead of a simple PLC/PIC based robot(liked Cybot or the RCX) or Beam, have a whole PC integrated into it. A common way is the "Lapbot", where a laptop is docked into a robot base with wheels and sensors. The upshot being that when you don't use the robot, you still have a functioning laptop.
